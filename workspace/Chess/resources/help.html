How to play


Top

The Window

The window is divided into four parts, the Ribbon, the Board, the Message View, and the Side view. Click on one of the view names or scroll down to navigate to the to the help section for each view.

Top

The Ribbon

The Ribbon contains the following buttons:

  • New Game: Opens a dialog in the Side view that asks you if you want to start a new game. If "Yes" is selected, the board and it's style are reset.
  • Settings: Opens a menu in the Side view that allows you to change the size of the window and the style for the pieces. You must click "Apply" to apply these changes.
  • Help: Opens the help screen in the Side view. Although I don't know how you're reading this if you haven't figured that out
  • Quit: Opens a dialog in the Side view that asks you if you want to quit. If "Yes" is selected, the application exits.
Top

The Board

The board shows all of the current pieces and their positions. To select a piece, click it. The square the piece is on and any squares the piece can move too are highlighted yellow. Click on one of those spaces to move the piece there.

Top

Message View

The message view is located below the Board and shows information such as whose turn it is, if someone is in check, and any other actions needed to be taken by the player.

Side View

The Ribbon

The Side view is blank initially, but can be populated by clicking one of the buttons in The Ribbon


Game Pieces


Top

King

Movement

The King can move only one space in any direction--vertically, horizontally or diagonally. It cannot move to or be in any position in which it can be captured the following turn or to a space occupied by a friendly piece.

Castling: If there are no pieces between The King and either one of the Rooks and neither piece has moved, you may move the King two spaces towards the Rook and move the Rook to the other side of the King. This cannot be done if the King is in Check.

Check and Checkmate

If the King is in a position to be captured, it is in check. You cannot end your turn with your King in check. If your King is in check and no moves are available, the game is over and you lose.

Top

Queen

Movement

The Queen can move in any direction--vertically, horizontally or diagonally--in a straight line until it reaches the end of the board or another piece.

Often, when a Pawn is promoted by moving to the other side of the board, it is promoted to a Queen.

Top

Rook

Movement

The Rook can move vertically or horizontally in a straight line until it reaches the end of the board or another piece

Castling: If there are no pieces between The King and either one of the Rooks and neither piece has moved, you may move the King two spaces towards the Rook and move the Rook to the other side of the King. This cannot be done if the King is in Check.

Top

Bishop

Movement

The Bishop can move diagonally in a straight line until it reaches the end of the board or another piece.

Top

Knight

Movement

The Knight can either move horizontally two squares and vertically one square, or vertically two squares and horizontally one square.

The Knight is the only piece that can "hop over" other pieces.

Top

Pawn

Movement

The Pawn can only one one space forward at a time, with the exception of its first move, in which it can move two spaces.

Capturing

The Pawn can usually only capture opposing pieces diagonally

En Passant: If an opposing pawn moves two spaces forward to an adjacent square to your pawn, you may capture the pawn as if it had only moved one square. This must be done on the very next turn.

Promotion

If a Pawn moves to the opposite end of the board, you may replace that Pawn with any other piece.
